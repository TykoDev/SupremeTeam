# Example Invocations

## Example 1

**User request:** research this problem space

**Output:**
- Actors: operations manager, dispatcher, field technician, and finance reviewer.
- Findings: the strongest needs are schedule confidence and evidence capture, not just route optimization.
- Next move: pass the packet to planning with clear workflow constraints and unresolved offline-sync questions.

## Example 2

**User request:** gather requirement evidence

**Output:**
- Evidence set: existing support tickets, compliance notes, and two comparable onboarding flows.
- Constraint: the product must support delegated approval without exposing full billing access.
- Design note: leave the exact permission model open, but treat role separation as a hard requirement.

## Example 3

**User request:** analyze stakeholder needs

**Output:**
- Conflict: sales wants a one-click setup path while operations requires auditable review steps.
- Research conclusion: the design must support a fast happy path plus explicit approval checkpoints, not choose one at the expense of the other.
- Escalation: validate the acceptable approval burden with the user before architecture locks the flow.
